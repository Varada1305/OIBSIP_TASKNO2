Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Born: 15 October 1931

Died: 27 July 2015

About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the most respected figures in Indian science and technology. His leadership and vision were instrumental in India's space and missile development programs. He served as the 11th President of India, a position that he used to inspire millions of young Indians to dream big and pursue excellence. What I admire most about Dr. Kalam is his unshakable belief in the power of education and his relentless dedication to improving the lives of people. His simplicity, humility and strong work ethic made him a true role model. He believed in the potential of every individual and continuously advocated for youth empowerment and innovation.

Dr. Kalam's famous book, "Wings of Fire," is a source of motivation and inspiration. His life continues to be an example of how one person can bring about positive change and inspire an entire nation.

Dr. Kalam's legacy will continue to inspire generations to come.
